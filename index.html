About

Broadcast is a role-playing game in which you will direct, produce and star in your own television series. Each player will create a character that is the star of their own story-line. You will explore a major story arc for your character each season with each season lasting for a single session.

Game Rules

The Basics

What you’ll Need

  • A bunch of note cards. (just bring the whole pack)
  • Some other human beings. A few or more but, less than lots.
  • Writing utensils. Enough to share.
  • A handful of 6-sided dice

Setup

  1. Pitch your show Follow the rules to create a pitch.
  2. Assemble your Crew Create crew cards for the following roles: Producer, Director, Writer. Just write the roles on some blank cards. Grab extra blank cards for each player above three. Shuffle all the cards and deal them out.
  3. Start Production You are ready to begin filming. Follow the filming directions until everyone has had a chance to try out all the roles a few times.
  4. Mid-Season break Take a 5 minute break. Then, shuffle the role cards as in step 2. discuss plot twist? highlight new actions
  5. Resume Production repeat step 3
  6. Plot Twist do the plot twist

Filming

  1. (director) previously… Your left card left the viewers wanting more. Remind them why they are excited to see this scene.
  2. (producer) focus The producer chooses a theme for this scene.
  3. (writer) snag The director chooses a snag from the focus in step 2. hand this card to the writer
  4. (director) choose Place the snag in front of you (not in a pile). Choose one:
    • Choose a pile
    • Choose an action. write it on a new card.
  5. (producer) choose The producer then performs the option not chosen in step 4
  6. (writer) engagement roll Make an roll for the character with the chosen action. If the action roll is a failure the writer should turn the current action card sideways.
  7. (director) action Play out the scene. Cast the other players to play the other roles in your scene. You overcome the snag in this scene; however, if the roll in step 6 was a failure there is a major cost or consequence.
    • If the left pile was chosen:
      1. play the scene overcoming the current snag with the action.
      2. place the action on top of the left pile (keep orientation)
      3. place the snag on top of the left pile
    • If the right pile was chosen:
      1. play the scene overcoming the snag on the right pile with the current action.
      2. place the action on top of the right pile (keep orientation)
      3. place the snag on top of the right pile
  8. (director) next time… A quick glimpse into the future foreshadowed by your top right card. One or two sentences.
  9. (all) pass Everyone with a role card passes it one player to the right.

Playing a Scene

  1. establish the stakes
  2. set the scene Choose one.
    • slug-line Write down a place and time. These can be exact or relative details
    • establishing shot Describe what we see to set the context before the scene unfolds.
  3. set the roles Figure out all the roles for your scene. You may discuss intents or circumstances with the other players. Each player ultimately has authority over their own character.
  4. assemble your actors Assign each role to a Player. Players may have more than one role. Players should always play their own character.
  5. action find out what happens.

If you need a character that doesn’t already belong to player, you have two options.

  • make one Grab an index card write down a name and a quick description. Set that card down in the middle for everyone to see. Something like Doris, The elderly bank-teller works fine.
  • take one Take a character from those previously made. Need an innocent bystander during your convenience store robbery? Just put Doris, The elderly bank-teller in harms way.

This game takes some liberties with the linearity of time. As such, finalities such as death can lead to some awkward situations. Here are some ways to avoid the confusion which might be cause by reconciling disparate stories.

  • don’t Let things not make sense. Shows have plot holes all the time. It’s okay!
  • the cliff hanger Literally and figuratively, use a cliffhanger. Don’t chop off someones head when you can kick them over the edge of a cliff and walk away.
  • a wizard did it Crazy things happen in the real world. Crazier things happen in an imaginary world. Perhaps some fundamental assumption you made about the world is just wrong. Maybe death isn’t so final or maybe you aren’t as good at killing as you thought.

The Pitch

Trailer

This is how your viewers first see your show. It should grab their attention and make them want to watch.

Focus

Focus is a topic of interest which exists in the context. Often these are the things agents might worry about. Each focus also has an inevitable outcome or, what would happen if no agents disrupted the focus.

Snag

Obstacles provide something which must be overcome in order for an agent to achieve their goal. Each obstacle is related to a single focus.

Action

Actions are the means that agents may employ to overcome obstacles.

Title

What you call the show.

Default actions are
Attune
Command
Consort
Finesse
Hunt
Prowl
Skirmish
Study
Survey
Sway
Tinker
Wreck

Create a Pitch

  1. (all) Choose actions. Create a list of actions by following the prompts use the default actions
  2. (all) Create the trailer. Imagine you must create a 15 second trailer for the show. Trailers don’t answers questions they make viewers ask them. What do the viewers see and hear?
  3. (each) create a focus. If two focuses are similar, rewrite them so the difference is clear and interesting or, combine them and create another.
  4. (each) create a character.
  5. (each) For each focus, create one snag, After each player has created a snag, go around once and let players add an additional snag to a focus of their choice.
  6. (all) title the show
  7. (all) Take five

The Characters

Vice

The characters’s guiding principle.

Strength

An action which the character is proficient at using to overcome obstacles.

Weakness

An action which the character has difficulty using to overcome obstacles.

Making action rolls

If the result of an action roll is 4+ it is a success; otherwise, it is a failure. For most action rolls simply roll 1d6. If the roll is made for an action that the character has a strength in, roll 2d6 and use the highest result. If the roll is made for an action that the character has a weakness in, roll 2d6 and use the lowest result.

Creating a character

  1. name your character. write on a new card
  2. Choose a vice. related to a focus. write on character card
  3. choose one strength from action list. write on character card
  4. choose one weakness from action list. write on character card
  5. place character card in front of you on your left
  6. create your character’s agenda write on a new card
  7. place agenda card in front of you on your right
