About

Broadcast is a role-playing game in which you will direct, produce and star in your own television series. Each player will create a character that is the star of their own story-line. You will explore a major story arc for your character each season with each season lasting for a single session.

Time

Time isn’t exactly linear in this game. On the same day one player may
shoot a scene for episode 2 while another player is shooting episode
16, It’s fine. There is only one fixed point in time for this game and
that lies somewhere between your piles. Everything to the left is
before that point and everything to the right is after that point. How
much before or after is up to you. Try to think of of it like this,
your left pile are are scenes that have already been shot but still
need to go to post. Your right pile is still being written, this is a
draft of the script and it will go that way if your character gets its
way but, things are subject to changes. In this game post isn’t done
on a computer its done through conversation. If something doesn’t make
sense try to talk out how you can use the footage you have to make it
work. If that can’t be done, do a re-shoot. Play out a pivotal scene
again or make a new one to see how it turns out.

Game Rules

The Basics

The Game

is a game for 3-5 players

What you’ll Need

  • A bunch of note cards. (just bring the whole pack)
  • Writing utensils
  • A handful of 6-sided dice
  • Lots of tokens. Anything you have a lot of. (I use my change jar)

Setup

  1. Pitch your show
  2. Outline the Characters
  3. Assemble your Crew Create crew cards for the following roles: Producer, Director, Writer
  4. Start Production

During a Scene

  1. Previously… Your left card left the viewers wanting more. Remind them why they are excited to see this scene.
  2. Choose Theme The producer chooses a theme for this scene.
  3. Choose an Obstacle The player with the “Choose an obstacle” marker will select an obstacle from the previously selected focus. Pass the obstacle to the “Active Player”
  4. Active Player Place the received obstacle in front of you (not in a pile). Choose one:
    • Choose a pile
    • Choose an action
  5. **** The player to left then performs the option not chosen in step 3
  6. Coming Soon Your right card will happen. Give a glimpse into the future or foreshadow some event
  7. Pass Roles Everyone with a role card passes it one player to the right.

The Pitch

Theme

Focus is a topic of interest which exists in the context. Often these are the things agents might worry about. Each focus also has an inevitable outcome or, what would happen if no agents disrupted the focus.

Obstacle

Obstacles provide something which must be overcome in order for an agent to achieve their goal. Each obstacle is related to a single focus.

Action

Actions are the means that agents may employ to overcome obstacles.

Default actions are
Attune
Command
Consort
Finesse
Hunt
Prowl
Skirmish
Study
Survey
Sway
Tinker
Wreck

Create a Pitch

  1. All players should decide on a context statement
  2. Each player creates a focus. 2.
  3. For each focus, each play creates one obstacle. Each player should make 3 copies of each obstacle and place them in a pile next to the associated focus.

The Agents

Agents are a force of change. Players have authority over their own agents.

Virtue

The agent’s guiding principle.

wealth, freedom,

Strength

An action which the character is proficient at using to overcome obstacles.

Weakness

An action which the character has difficulty using to overcome obstacles.
